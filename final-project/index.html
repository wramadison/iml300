Project 2: HyperNarrative

Final: Smile for the Camera

This hypernarrative is all about me. I like to think of it as both a small photo album documentary of my life through various random photos, as well as my rather rocky relationship with photos and cameras and how I, being someone who hates being photographed or recorded, ironically fell in love with being on the other side of the camera. The journey of the story talks about the different types of pictures my family have taken of me and treasure, from baby photos, to pictures with fake smiles, to performance photos, to candid shots. As I explain the types of photos as well as the specific examples I use, I wanted the user to get a better understanding of my relationship with cameras.

Project 3: Networked Justice

Working at Profcorp Inc.:A Professionalism Translator

This project is about workplace discrimination. The small journey the user takes as a new employee at PROFCORP INc., a majority-white company, discloses many of the typical forms of harrassment or discrimination that workers of various marginalized identites face in majority-white workspaces. The concept of workplace professionalism and workplace culture also typically masks this discriminatory behavior, and I highlighted that as well.

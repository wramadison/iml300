About: Workplace Discrimination

The corporate workplace can be a cesspool for harrassment and discrimination under the guise of maintaining professionalism.

Out of approximately 60,000 workplace complaints reported last year, 42% of them were tied to one's race or ethnicity. This doesn't account for those afraid to file complaints due to possible retaliation, the most common form of workplace harrassment.

Discrimination via workplace "professionalism" looks like:
  • Microaggressive comments and actions
  • Requiring tattoos or piercings to be covered
  • Stringent and exclusive limitations on professional attire
  • Use of exclusionary language and tones
  • Prohibiting conversations on diversity, deeming them "too political"
  • Directly or indirectly forcing POC employees to "speak White" or "speak proper English"
  • Creating a predatory or biased "work culture"
